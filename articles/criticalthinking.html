Critical Thinking

2026-04-09

Before knowing what critical thinking is, let's see what a fact, guess, & opinion are.

Fact → Something that is true, provable with evidence, and remains the same no matter who observes it. Example: Earth orbits the Sun.

Guess → Something assumed without enough evidence, often quick or intuitive, and likely to be wrong. Example: “It might rain today” (without checking weather).

Opinion → A personal view or interpretation, sometimes based on facts, but not universally true. Example: “This movie is boring.”

Critical thinking → Carefully evaluating before believing, by checking evidence, questioning, thinking logically, and staying open to change.

Let's see critical thinking with one example:

Situation: You see the “Coffee is bad for your health” line somewhere.

  • Analysis (Understand) → What is being said?
    • Claim = Coffee is harmful. Why = Not clearly stated (health risk assumed).
  • Evaluation (Check) → Is this true or reliable?
    • Source = Could be news headline or someone’s opinion. Evidence = Scientific studies show mixed results. Logic = “Bad for everyone” is too general.
  • Inference (Decide) → What can I conclude?
    • Coffee is not universally bad. Moderate intake may even have benefits. Effect depends on person, quantity, and condition.
  • Reflection (Self-check) → Am I biased or assuming something?
    • If I like coffee → I may ignore negative facts. If I dislike coffee → I may accept negative claims quickly.
  • Open-mindedness (Stay Flexible) → Can I change my mind if I’m wrong?
    • If strong medical evidence shows harm → I will adjust my view. Stay balanced: neither blindly trust nor reject.

Overthinking: Uncontrolled, repetitive, and unproductive thinking without reaching a decision.

Endnote: Use critical thinking when the impact is high or the truth is uncertain—it leads to clear decisions and breaks overthinking loops.

Quick self-check:

  • Moving toward a clear conclusion → Critical thinking.
  • Stuck in repeated thoughts without progress → Overthinking.
