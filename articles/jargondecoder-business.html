Jargon Decoder - Business

2026-04-27

  • Inflation → The general rise in prices of goods and services over time, reducing what your money can purchase → Last year ₹100 filled your grocery bag. This year the same ₹100 fills only half of it.
  • Recession → When a country's economic output (GDP) falls for two or more consecutive quarters. Businesses slow, unemployment rises. → The whole market shuts down — not just one shop. Sellers have goods, buyers have no money.
  • GDP → Gross Domestic Product — the total value of all goods and services produced in a country in a year. The country's economic report card → If India were a family, GDP is the total income earned by every member combined in a year.
  • Subsidy → Money given by the government to reduce the cost of a product or service for citizens or businesses→ Your LPG cylinder costs ₹900 but the government secretly pays ₹200 — you pay only ₹700.
  • Tariff → A tax imposed by a government on goods coming in from another country — makes imports more expensive to protect local industry → A toll gate at the border — every foreign truck pays extra before it can enter and sell.
  • Monopoly → When a single company dominates an entire market with no real competition — giving them full control over price and supply → Only one water supplier in your colony — you pay whatever they charge or stay thirsty.
  • Fiscal Deficit → When a government's total expenditure exceeds its total revenue in a given year. It then borrows to cover the gap → Your monthly salary is ₹50,000 but you spent ₹65,000 — you borrowed ₹15,000 on a credit card.
  • Privatisation → When the government transfers ownership of a state-run company to private businesses or individuals → The municipality-run canteen was losing money — they sold it to a private owner who now runs it profitably.
  • Interest Rate → The percentage charged on a loan or paid on savings. Set by the RBI in India — affects home loans, car loans, everything → You borrow ₹1 lakh. At 8% interest you return ₹1.08 lakh — the extra ₹8,000 is the "rent" on the money.
  • Austerity → When a government cuts public spending — on welfare, infrastructure, salaries — to reduce debt and balance its books → Family is in debt — they cancel the DTH, stop eating out, and the kids switch to a government school.
