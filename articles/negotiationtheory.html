Negotiation Theory

2026-04-05

In most life situations, instead of thinking in terms of winning or losing, we can approach them like a test match—where the goal is to win or at least secure a draw (a win-win outcome), rather than accept defeat.

Real-World Parallels

  • Psychology
    • Win-Win Mindset (Abundance Thinking): People believe outcomes can benefit both sides.
    • Conflict Resolution: The focus shifts from “Who wins?” to “How can both benefit?”
    • Emotional Regulation: Not reacting aggressively → opens room for better outcomes.
  • Philosophy
    • Game theory (Non-Zero-Sum Games): Not all situations are win-lose. Both parties can gain (like co-operation scenarios).
    • Stoicism: Focus on what you can control. Avoid seeing everything as victory vs defeat.
    • Pragmatism: Best outcome = what works for all involved, not absolute winning.

Limitations

  • True competition: Knockout match → one winner.
  • Scarce resources: Limited jobs, seats, money.
  • Ethical conflicts: Some situations require taking a clear side.
